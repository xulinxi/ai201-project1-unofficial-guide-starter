ORIGINAL POST:
Hey! I'm an incoming international grad student. I've been comparing grocery prices and omg everything is so expensive. So I've been wondering if it's cheaper to get the meal plan/blocks than meal prepping? A bunch of scared grad international students will truly appreciate your thoughts on this. Thanks!

COMMENTS:

[score 3] Life hack: if you do choose to do the meal plan, choose the smallest one and sneak into the dining halls. If you get caught, say you forgot to swipe in and use one of your 5 swipes for the week. Most of the time you won't get caught. This only works at certain dining halls.

[score 1] You can cook meals for less than RDE meal prices. Bulk prep helps. Trader Joes and Costco have good prices, Costco delivers to campus. You might also want to try out a meal block (can purchase a 5 meal block, or more) and see what you think. Most grad students cook their own meals. I would not commit to a full meal plan before you figure out your routine, chances are you won't use them all. But try both at first (cook for yourself, purchase some meals) and you'll soon know what works best. There's also a monthly food pantry pop-up for students that qualify, offering some groceries at no cost.

[score 1] You can buy meal blocks instead of paying for a meal plan. Lunch is like $12/meal if you buy 10 meals or more. You can refund your meal blocks later if you don't manage to use them up! Also, no one has mentioned Tupperware...

[score 1] Get a meal plan! It's cheaper than going out, and if you happen to have extras leftover before the next academic year you can request a refund (minus a $15 fee which is worth the savings of buying a larger block). Without a car you don't want to get into a habit of getting food delivered.

[score 1] Grocery shopping is also very doable without a car or delivery app:
- From on campus housing, Real Produce is walking distance, and Trader Joes in Town and Country, Grocery Outlet and Safeway are all an easy bicycle ride away
- If shuttles are more your speed, there's also a weekday Marguerite shuttle to the TJ's in Town and Country, and a weekend shuttle to San Antonio Shopping Center, which has a Walmart, TJ's and Safeway.

[score 1] If you have no car but are consistent with buying groceries, Safeway has a delivery plan that is definitely worth it. It's $90/year but gives you $5/month to use and isn't marked up like Instacart. Otherwise, Grocery Outlet... don't hate me plz.

[score 1] For groceries, Instacart is worth the fee if you can't borrow a vehicle. It's $10/month (which you can split with someone else) for unlimited free deliveries from a ton of stores, delivered in a 1-2 hour timeframe. They mark up the price slightly at some stores; for Costco fresh food deliveries it's like 5%. You also do not need a Costco membership if you order through Instacart. Perhaps combine the two: split Instacart with a roommate and go in on Costco orders together. You would save a TON of money.

[score 1] I found it helpful to have a meal plan for busy weekdays, especially since dining halls are central, and campus is so big and it can take time to head home/eat/head back. I tended to cook for myself on weekends. There are also on campus restaurants (so you don't have to commit to a meal plan) with longer hours (more flexibility), but the dining halls tend to have more healthy options (steamed fish, large salad bars). Not too many grad students had the meal plan imo, but a good number definitely did. I ended up doing a blend of the three (meal plan, on campus restaurants and cooking at home).

[score 1] Your time is very precious as a grad student. So having a meal plan is super helpful especially during those busy times when cooking just isn't an option. Also dining halls are a good way to meet your cohort / labmates especially at the beginning.. u wanna be in the loop. That said, I'd still recommend cooking most of your meals if you can haha. Trader Joe's and Costco are perfect. Good luck :)

[score 1] Will you have access to a car? Grocery shopping is doable with access to a car. There are zipcars available around campus but that adds to your costs of course. There is a mini store available on campus but food pricing is high. In the end, cooking for yourself is cheaper but you have to factor time in for shopping, prepping and cleaning given your workload and any part time job you might have. Campus food is good and nutritional. The dining halls offer many different food options. Check out the dining halls online and see if the menu items would work for you.

[score 1] There are A LOT of receptions after lectures and a lot of "info session" lunches that almost always serve way too much food. You can save money by going to these and eating and taking food home with you.

[score 1] It's time to find out that you actually don't have to have three full meals a day to survive. When people are really on a budget, they can always find the calories they need around campus without either meal plan or grocery :)

[score 1] Uber eats and instacart. Dining hall food is absolute dogshit and expensive.
