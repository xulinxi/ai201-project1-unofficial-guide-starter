ORIGINAL POST:
My housing draw partner and I, both rising sophomores, were assigned to Yost House, which is the theme house for French/Italian/Slavic languages.

Will we need to participate in any of the language/culture traditions as residents of the house? Additionally, is the house culture dominated by those who participate in these language communities or is it more like any other dorm?

COMMENTS:

[score 12] Decades ago, as an incoming freshman, I was randomly assigned to a theme dorm that I had no relation to. It was fine/fun. $1 says by the end of the year you'll participate in a few cultural events just for the hell of it.

[score 8] Short answer: NO! From the R&DE Housing Application email sent a month or so ago: "Students assigned to academic theme communities or ethnic theme houses through this process are not required to take part in theme programming or requirements. Students assigned to co-ops through this process will be required to participate in the house cooking and cleaning duties. You are not required to list the theme communities on your application."
